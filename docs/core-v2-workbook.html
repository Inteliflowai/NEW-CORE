The Core Loop (the thing that must work)

Locked

Locked: Notice → Act → Confirm. Teacher notices who needs help (5-second test), acts (check-in / targeted practice / reteach), and later confirms whether it worked (did mastery move?). The whole product serves this loop.

The Generation Engine (your functionality spec)

Needs you

This is the heart of CORE. One pipeline, every piece feeding the next:

Lesson Plan → Quiz (3 MCQ + 2 OEQ) → Read cognitive + behavior signals → Set CL + detect Learning Strategies → generate Differentiated Assignment + Spark Challenge + Super TELI support — Strategies & Power Skills baked in.
  • Lesson plan. One sentence or one upload in — CORE writes the full plan, passage, objectives, key concepts. Review, edit, publish.
  • Quiz: 3 MCQ + 2 OEQ. MCQs read comprehension fast. The 2 open-ended questions are the engine — reasoning, critical thinking, and the specific misconception live there. AI-graded with a rubric, eval-gated from day one.
  • Grade-anchored difficulty. Questions are generated to the student's grade level — a Grade 6 item and a Grade 12 item on the same skill are not the same difficulty. This is a hard generation constraint, not an afterthought.
  • Adaptive once there's data (resolved). It's not fixed-vs-adaptive — it's sequenced. Cold-start is a clean fixed 3 MCQ + 2 OEQ at grade level. Once CORE has enough history on a student, Q4–Q5 reshape in real time off Q1–Q3 — scaffolding down or challenging up.
  • CL = Reinforce / On Track / Enrich. Three states, each a teacher verb. Set per skill, not per student.
  • LS detection from the 12 Strategies. Quiz seeds LS weakly; behavior signals confirm it over time. Never a day-one verdict.
  • CL drives generation: Reinforce → scaffolded work + more Super TELI. On Track → grade-level. Enrich → Spark / stretch + Socratic-only Super TELI.
  • Media-rich outputs. Every passage, question, and hint can be read aloud, students can speak back, and assignments include generated illustrations and diagrams. See Section 5.

Your decisions: (a) Confirm grade-anchored difficulty, and set the data threshold at which Q4–Q5 start adapting (how many attempts/quizzes before reshape kicks in). (b) Confirm CL is per-skill. (c) What is “Super” about Super TELI vs today's Teli?

5

Media: Audio & Visuals

Needs you

CORE's content is never text-only. Audio and visuals are core to accessibility (struggling readers, ELL, younger grades) and engagement — not decoration.

  • Audio — playing (read-aloud / TTS). Passages, questions, and Super TELI's hints can be spoken aloud. Built in, every tier.
  • Audio — listening (voice). Students speak to Super TELI; speech is transcribed. Voice only on non-reading tasks — same rule as LIFT, so we never read a reading-comprehension answer aloud.
  • Visuals — illustrations. AI-generated images bring lessons and assignments to life.
  • Visuals — diagrams. Auto-generated diagrams (processes, structures, concept maps) where a picture beats a paragraph.
  • Video. Richer generated media for select content — heavier cost, so Pro+.
  • Tier-gated limits. Generous monthly AI-media allowances (illustrations, video, voice, TTS) on Essentials; Pro raises every ceiling. These limits double as the cost guardrail (Section 14).

Your decisions: which media are in the Essentials MVP vs Pro? Confirm voice-on-non-reading-tasks. Set the monthly limit numbers per tier.

Look & Feel — and the Discipline Around It

Needs you

The visual direction (new for v2): ditch V1's look entirely. v2 is vibrant, energetic, pop-art-influenced — bold color, confident type, playful motion. The opposite of the sterile ed-tech dashboard.

But intensity by surface — this is the part most people get wrong:

  • Student screens: full pop-art energy. Bold blocks, saturated color, big friendly type, playful micro-animations, Super TELI's character. This drives engagement and matches the gamification.
  • Teacher / parent / admin screens: same DNA, dialed for credibility. Confident and modern, never cartoonish. A head of school evaluating CORE must take it seriously — a clownish teacher dashboard kills the sale.
  • Bold skin, disciplined bones. Spend the boldness on a signature element; keep the information design calm so the 5-second test still works. Pop-art look + restrained data is the whole trick.
  • Vibrant ≠ unreadable. Bold color must still hit WCAG AA contrast.
  • Avoid the generic AI look. Pick a specific, intentional palette — not stock Memphis squiggles.

Starter tokens to react to (not final): an electric palette — vivid violet + hot coral + acid lime accents over clean near-white, deep ink for text — or a louder, higher-energy evolution of your current indigo/amber. Expressive geometric display face + a highly legible body face. Motion with purpose: a satisfying beat when work is graded, a celebration when a student climbs.

The rest of “better” isn't more features — it's discipline:

  • Time-to-first-value under 5 minutes. A teacher's first session must produce one real differentiated assignment.
  • 30-minute implementation. GC imports the roster, students launch with no new login, demo class pre-loaded. Make it a design requirement.
  • Defaults over settings. Pre-fill class, subject, due date. Nothing blocks the teacher.
  • One screen, one job, one action. The thing that broke V1. Hold the line.
  • Healthy engagement, not casino mechanics. Keep XP/streaks/badges, but make “You vs 4 weeks ago” the default and the leaderboard off by default.
  • Plain language is the product. The parent-narrative voice extends to teacher and student screens.
  • Trust as a feature. Observational-not-diagnostic, no scary labels, school-controlled deletion.
  • Mobile + accessible by default. Teachers check phones; read-aloud and keyboard nav built in.

Your decisions: Does vibrant replace indigo/amber, or build on it? Confirm the student-loud / adult-credible split. Leaderboard off by default? Pick the one “first 5 minutes” flow to obsess over.

How We'll Know It Has Value

Needs you

V1 lesson: “We don't even know if it has value” — because we never defined it upfront.

Leading signs:

  • Teachers log in weekly without nagging.
  • Teachers take a CORE-recommended action (the click that proves the insight landed).
  • Login to useful insight under ~30 seconds; first differentiated assignment under 5 minutes.

Proof it worked:

  • Flagged students' assignment grades trend up.
  • The Assignment-vs-Quiz gap narrows for students we intervened on.
  • Teachers say, unprompted, “this changed what I did in class.”
