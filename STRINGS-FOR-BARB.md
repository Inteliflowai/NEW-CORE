## 1. Highest leverage — one shared default, 7 teacher screens

The shared `EmptyState` "just-getting-started" default body is **student-addressed
language shown to teachers** across Gradebook, Alerts, High Fives, Insights, Lesson
Library, Quiz Library, and Upload. A teacher staring at an empty screen has no idea
what populates it (student practice, not their own action) or what to do meanwhile.
Fix once at the call sites with per-screen overrides.

**File:** `src/components/core/EmptyState.tsx` (default) → per-screen overrides

| Screen | Current (shared default) | Proposed draft |
|---|---|---|
| *(default)* | "Keep going — more practice builds a clearer picture." | *(stop showing the student line to teachers; use the per-screen copy below)* |
| Gradebook | — | "Grades show up here as students finish assignments. Nothing to do yet." |
| Alerts | — | "No alerts right now — your class is on track. Check back after students do more practice." |
| High Fives | — | "No wins to celebrate yet — when a student breaks through, it lands here." |
| Insights | — | "Patterns appear as your class completes more practice. Check back after a few sessions." |
| Lesson / Quiz Library | — | "Nothing here yet. Upload or create one and it shows up here." |
| Upload | — | "Drop a lesson, quiz, or resource here to build your library." |

**Why:** Speaks-first, in the teacher's voice. (Rule 1 + Rule 3.)

## 2. Teacher daily surfaces — words over numbers (register)

The two highest-traffic helpers lead with raw percentages and point-gaps. The
*construct* may stay teacher-side, but the *numbers* put CORE back in the analyst's
chair. Exact figures can live behind a detail view if a teacher wants them.

**File:** `src/lib/copy/triageWhySentence.ts`

- **Current:** "Quiz average is 48% and runs about 22 points below their assignment scores — when assignment and quiz results split this far apart and quizzes are this low, the concept itself usually needs another pass with the group."
  **Proposed:** "Their quiz results are noticeably lower than their assignment work — when the two drift this far apart and quizzes are this low, the concept itself usually needs another pass with the group."

- **Current:** "Assignment average (61%) is about 18 points below their quiz average (79%) — they're doing better on quizzes than on the assignments themselves, so they may understand more than the work shows. A quick verbal check confirms it before you act."
  **Proposed:** "They're scoring noticeably higher on quizzes than on assignments — they may understand more than the work shows. A quick verbal check confirms it before you act." *(keeps the suggest-then-confirm tail — exactly the posture)*

- **Current:** "Their assignment and quiz scores diverge by about 14 points — noticeably stronger on one than the other, which is worth a closer look at what's going on for them."
  **Proposed:** "Their assignment and quiz results tell noticeably different stories — worth a closer look at what's going on for them." *("divergence" is on the banned list even when softened)*

- **Current:** "A 8-point gap between their assignment and quiz work — a small divergence for now, but worth keeping an eye on."
  **Proposed:** "Their assignment and quiz work are drifting slightly apart — small for now, but worth keeping an eye on."

**File:** `src/lib/copy/divergencePhrase.ts` (student drill-in)

- **Current:** "Assignment scores (74%) are running about 20 points above quiz scores (54%). The work looks stronger than the quizzes — worth checking whether the assignment results hold up unaided."
  **Proposed:** "Their assignment work looks noticeably stronger than their quiz results — worth checking whether it holds up unaided."

- **Current:** "Quiz scores (79%) are running about 18 points above assignment scores (61%). They may understand more than the assignment work shows."
  **Proposed:** "Their quiz results are running noticeably higher than their assignment work — they may understand more than the assignments show."

**File:** `src/app/(teacher)/students/[studentId]/page.tsx`

- **Current:** "The reteach is paying off — keep going. (+12 pts)"
  **Proposed:** "The reteach is paying off — keep going." *(drop the delta, or render a directional word: "a notable jump" / "a small step up")*

**Why (all of the above):** Rule 3 — words over numbers; the sentence already states the meaning, the number only adds analyst register.

## Quiz Runner — Teli score-message register (FLAG for Barb, from Phase-3 review)

The V1-ported Teli post-quiz messages (`src/lib/quiz/scoreMessage.ts`) are shown to STUDENTS on the done screen. Several variants carry band/diagnosis register that passes the numeric leak-guard but leans against the coach-posture language standard ("Mastery" not "Band"; plain words, no diagnosis):

| Current (draft, student-facing) | Concern | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| "Top-band score" / "Mid-band score" | "Band" is a banned word | plain encouragement, no "band" |
| "Above grade level" | grade-relative framing | "you vs your own past", growth-framed |
| "Reteach mode" / "Reteach scope" | "Reteach" is a teacher diagnosis | warm, forward-looking ("let's revisit…") |

**Recommendation:** Barb to rewrite these variants in plain coach voice; consider extending `leakGuard` to catch banned *words* (band/reteach/divergence/…), not only numbers, so the diagnostic-wall is enforced on register too.
